Logos and Icons

Though I am by no means an expert in graphic design, I do periodically enjoy creating logos for the various courses I have taught and programs I am a part of. Below are a few of the designs I have created; hover over them for a brief description!

PSTAT 120A

The dice and cards symbolize the “famous” aspects of probability, and the background displays an illustration of the Weak Law of Large Numbers.

PSTAT 120B

The dice symbolize the first part of this course (Probability), and the background depicts an illustration of the Central Limit Theorem as applied to the Gamma Distribution.

PSTAT 100

I feel this one is a bit self-explanatory: there’s data, and there’s science!

UCSB-Keio Exchange Program

This logo combines UCSB’s famous Storke Tower with a traditional Japanese Shinto Shrine, symbolizing the bridge between UCSB and Keio University that this program provides.

SBSI

UCSB is symbolized by Storke Tower, the ocean, and palm trees while statistics is symbolized by the “constellation” of data points in the sky.

LaTeX

If you are planning on pursuing any STEM-related field, I highly recommend that you learn LaTeX. LaTeX is a markup language used primarily to generate mathematical documents, and includes extensive support for typesetting equations. Overleaf (which, fun fact, was called “ShareLaTeX” back when I started using it) is an online interface which can be used to generate LaTeX documents without having to download any software on your computer, and also provides a very useful “Learn LaTeX in 30 minutes” guide.
